Schedules

Our purpose in our school schedule is to relieve students and to make them want to come to school everyday. One big problem with kids in schools is the reluctance to learn. This fun schedule will most likely appeal to parents, teachers, and students.

Weekly

Lots of schools today use the traditional 5-day week schedule, and we are keeping the same idea, but with a twist. On every Wednesday, teachers must give students fun activities that will still teach students valuable information. For example, teachers may host a jeopardy game, have students do amusing worksheets, or watch a movie. Wednesdays are in the middle of the week, so it gives kids a break from the sometimes seemingly endless week. The Wednesdays would also give teachers a break from speaking and can give them time to grade papers and such.

Yearly

The yearly school schedule will be like a normal school year, instead of year round, because at the end of the year (at the beginning of summer), students will have a sense of achievement and self-accomplishment. They will also get days off on the important holidays (Martin Luther King Day, Veterans Day, Memorial Day, etc.). Longer breaks help students enjoy their time off and to relieve stress.

Classroom

The purpose of this classroom is to have the students sit on the side with the blue carpet for lectures or when the teacher is giving instructions, then they sit in the three groups to work on projects. At the blue carpet, no one sits infront of another person, this encourages more communication and involvement. At the three groups, there would be computers of the students to work on, the computers' monitors will be tracked and able to be veiwed by the teacher at any time from his computer.
